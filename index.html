I am an Assistant Research Professor at Northeastern University with the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department. I received my PhD from Télécom ParisTech in electronics and communications under the guidance of David Gesbert while working at EURECOM in the south of France. My research interests include dynamic spectrum access, integrated sensing and communications (ISAC), positioning, emulation and testbeds for 5G and 6G, connected robotics, and RAN digital twins.

Research Areas

Positioning and Sensing with Cellular Networks

Advanced positioning and sensing techniques leveraging cellular infrastructure. I develop novel approaches for bistatic sensing, round trip time estimation, and high-precision localization using 5G NR signals, enabling centimeter-level accuracy for various applications.

  • R. Gangula, S. Velumani, and T. Melodia, "Bistatic Sensing in 5G NR," IEEE MILCOM, Los Angeles, CA, USA, 2025.
  • R. Gangula, T. Melodia, R. Mundlamuri, and F. Kaltenberger, "Round Trip Time Estimation Utilizing Cyclic Shift of Uplink Reference Signal," IEEE ICC, Montreal, Canada, 2025.
  • R. Mundlamuri, R. Gangula, F. Kaltenberger and R. Knopp, "Novel Round Trip Time Estimation in 5G NR," IEEE GLOBECOM, Cape Town, South Africa, 2024.

Spectrum Sharing

Advanced techniques for efficient spectrum utilization in wireless networks. I develop cognitive radio algorithms and spectrum sharing approaches that enable dynamic allocation of frequency bands based on real-time network conditions and user demands.

  • R. Gangula et al., "Listen-While-Talking: Toward dApp-based Real-Time Spectrum Sharing in O-RAN," IEEE MILCOM, Washington, DC, USA, 2024.

Connected Robotics

While having robust wireless connectivity is crucial for autonomous robots and UAV networks, these robots can also act as agents, enhancing wireless network performance and coverage. The research focuses on developing control and communication strategies, as well as experimental prototypes, in the context of connected robotics.

  • O. Esrafilian, R. Gangula, and D. Gesbert, “3D map-based trajectory design in UAV-aided wireless localization systems”, IEEE Internet of Things Journal, vol. 8, no. 12, pp. 9894-9904, 2021.
  • O. Esrafilian, R. Gangula, and D. Gesbert, “Learning to communicate in UAV-aided wireless networks: Map-based approaches”, IEEE Internet of Things Journal, vol. 6, no. 2, pp. 1791-1802, 2019.
  • R. Gangula, et.al “Flying rebots: First results on an autonomous UAV-based LTE relay using OpenAirInterface”, in Proc. SPAWC, Kalamata, Greece, 2018.

Experimental Testbeds

Prototyping and evaluating O-RAN and 5G/6G protocols, algorithms, and network architectures with experimental testbeds and emulation platforms. These platforms leverage open-source 3GPP-compliant cellular softwares such as OpenAirInterfce , software-defined radios, and large-scale network emulation platforms like Colosseum.

  • R. Mundlamuri, R. Gangula, F. Kaltenberger and R. Knopp, "5G NR Positioning with OpenAirInterface: Tools and Methodologies," IEEE WONS, Hintertux, Austria, 2025.
  • H. Cheng, S. D'Oro, R. Gangula et al, "ORANSlice: An Open Source 5G Network Slicing Platform for O-RAN," ACM MobiCom, Washington, D.C., USA, 2024.
  • R. Mundlamuri, S. Badran, R. Gangula, F. Kaltenberger, J. M. Jornet and T. Melodia, "5G Over Terahertz Using OpenAirInterface," IEEE WONS, Chamonix, France, 2024.
  • M. Polese, L. Bonati, S. D'Oro, P. Johari, D.Villa, S. Velumani, R. Gangula, et al, "Colosseum: The open RAN digital twin," IEEE Open Journal of the Communications Society, vol. 5, pp. 5452-5466, 2024.
